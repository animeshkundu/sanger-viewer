Annotations come to life: 6-frame ORFs and restriction sites

July 3, 2026

This step adds an annotation lane above the chromatogram so the sequence now carries biological context, not just signal shape. The viewer computes six-frame ORFs and common restriction-enzyme sites from the active strand, then shows them as keyboard-accessible chips grouped into ORF +/− frame rows and a restriction row.

What shipped

  • Pure TypeScript annotation engine for 6-frame ORF calling and enzyme-site scanning.
  • Support for common enzymes including EcoRI and non-palindromic BsaI with explicit cut offsets.
  • A viewport-windowed DOM track above the trace: only overlapping/nearby chips render.
  • Clickable + keyboard-accessible annotation chips that navigate the chromatogram to the selected range.
  • Cohesive light/dark styling using design tokens and focus-visible states.

Verification

Vitest now asserts exact ORF and restriction-site coordinates (including reverse-strand mapping and non-palindromic cut math). Playwright E2E verifies real annotation rendering, viewport-aware windowing, and non-pixel click/keyboard navigation behavior.

This keeps the v0–v11 foundation intact while moving the app closer to professional Sanger-viewer parity.
