2026-07-04 — v18 first-impression overhaul

July 4, 2026

Today’s change is about the first two seconds. The app used to open like it had crashed: dark chrome, white canvas, nothing rendered yet, and the actual payoff hidden behind a file picker. That is the wrong opening move for a chromatogram viewer. Now the page lands on a real sample trace immediately, the quality track is already alive, and the UI plainly says the sample is only there to help you get oriented before you drop in your own file.

What changed

  • The bundled sample.ab1 trace now auto-loads on first visit.
  • The chromatogram canvas background now follows the theme surface token instead of hardcoding white.
  • A dismissible ribbon explains that you are viewing a local sample and can drop in your own .ab1 or .scf file.
  • Fresh-load tests now assert known sample facts instead of relying on vague “canvas is non-empty” checks.

Why it matters

A first-time visitor should not have to imagine why the project is useful. They should see a rendered chromatogram, colored peaks, base calls, and quality information right away. The moment the page loads, the product now answers the question “why would I care?” with an actual example instead of an empty shell.
